Local Recycling Rules

Recycling rules vary by UK council. This page provides general guidance for students and young workers living in shared households.

General Recycling Rules

  • Rinse containers before recycling
  • Remove food residue from packaging
  • Do not bag recyclables in plastic bags (unless required by council)
  • Flatten cardboard boxes to save space
  • Check local council guidance for specific rules

What You CAN Recycle

  • Clean plastic bottles and containers
  • Cardboard and paper (dry and clean)
  • Glass bottles and jars (rinsed, lids off)
  • Metal cans and tins (rinsed)
  • Aluminium foil (clean and scrunched into a ball)

What You CANNOT Recycle (in most UK councils)

  • Food-soiled paper or cardboard (e.g. greasy pizza boxes)
  • Plastic bags and film (use supermarket collection points instead)
  • Broken glass or ceramics
  • Disposable nappies
  • Electrical items (use WEEE recycling points)
  • Batteries (use dedicated battery collection)

Food Waste & Composting

  • Most UK councils provide a food waste caddy
  • Fruit peels, vegetable scraps, eggshells, coffee grounds, tea bags are all compostable
  • Use compostable liners, not plastic bags

Finding Your Local Rules

  • Rules vary between councils
  • Visit gov.uk/recycling-collections to find your local council
  • Check your council website for bin collection days and specific rules
